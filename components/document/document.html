Semantic alignment in Chitimacha

Daniel W. Hieber

University of California, Santa Barbara

Introduction

This paper describes the alignment pattern for verbal person markers in Chitimacha (ISO 639-3: ctm; Glottolog: chit1248), a language isolate of Louisiana.[1] In accord with Mithun (1991: 537, 1999: 388, 2008: 328–329), Chitimacha is generally thought to follow an agent-patient alignment pattern in the first person on the basis of a handful of examples published in a brief grammatical sketch by Swadesh (1946). These examples are shown in (1) and (2), with glosses and analysis from Mithun (2008: 328–329).
